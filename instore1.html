        Why ecological gardens

        A garden can be
        infrastructure.

        An ecological garden doesn’t just look good — it functions. It builds soil, holds water, softens extremes, supports birds and insects, and improves with time.

        The goal is a garden that belongs: suited to site conditions, planted in layers, and designed to be maintained without fighting the climate.

        What clients usually want
        • Beauty that lasts
        • Low-stress maintenance
        • Shade + comfort outdoors
        • A garden that feels alive
        What the garden delivers
        • Habitat + biodiversity
        • Soil formation + resilience
        • Water holding + infiltration
        • Seasonal depth + calm
